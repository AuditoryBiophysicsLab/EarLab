BrugheraMSO2 The BrugheraMSO2 module simulates individual principal cells of the medial superior olive (MSO), which receive bilateral excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs, and have responses sensitive to the interaural time delay (ITD) of low-frequency auditory stimuli. Each instance of the module employs a single-compartment Hodgkin-Huxley neuron model derived for Type II ventral cochlear nucleus neurons, including expressions for IKLT, IKHT, INa, ILEAK, and Ih (Rothman and Manis 2003c) with characteristics that are likely similar to those in principal MSO cells (Svirskis et al. 2004; Scott et al. 2005).
Bushy Cell (Rothman93) This module (Rothman et al., 1993) is a single-compartment Hodgkin-Huxley neuron model that simulates the membrane activity and discharge patterns of a bushy cell in the ventral cochlear nucleus (VCN). The neural compartment is defined by an electric circuit equation incorporating the membrane currents associated with capacitance, leakage, ion channels, and synaptic inputs. The characteristics of the three voltage-sensitive ion-channels (fast sodium, delayed-rectifier high-threshold potassium, and low-threshold potassium) were derived from voltage-clamp data taken from the VCN (Manis and Marx, 1991). In addition to simulation of bushy cells, the Rothman et al. 1993 model is the basis for models of neurons in the medial superior olive (MSO) (Brughera, 1996) and inferior colliculus (Cai, 1998). Steady-state inhibition, used in the original Rothman 1993 model as a mechanism to produce variation in the inter-spike intervals in bushy cells of high CF, has been omitted in this module. Rothman and Manis (2003) have introduced an updated model with additional voltage-sensitive potassium channels and revised ion-channel dynamics. The 2003 model incorporates responses of chopper cells and bushy cells, and without inhibition it produces the physiologically observed variability in inter-spike intervals.
BushyCell2 (RothmanManis2003) The BushyCell2 module simulates the electrical activity of individual neurons of the ventral cochlear nucleus (VCN) (Rothman and Manis 2003c, 2003b, and 2003a). Each BushyCell2 instance incorporates a single-compartment Hodgkin-Huxley neuron model with expressions for IKA, IKLT, IKHT, INa, ILEAK, and Ih. The BushyCell2 module can simulate either bushy cells which project to the superior olivary complex or medial nucleus of the trapezoid body, or stellate cells which project to the inferior colliculus or dorsal cochlear nucleus.
BushyCell2 (RothmanManis2003) The BushyCell2 module simulates the electrical activity of individual neurons of the ventral cochlear nucleus (VCN) (Rothman and Manis 2003c, 2003b, and 2003a). Each BushyCell2 instance incorporates a single-compartment Hodgkin-Huxley neuron model that incorporates expressions for currents IKA, IKLT, IKHT, INa, ILEAK, and Ih. In the HRC we often employ this module to simulate bushy cells, which project from the VCN to the medial superior olive or medial nucleus of the trapezoid body. In addition this module captures the equal facility provided by the Rothman and Manis model (2003c) for simulating stellate cells, which project to the inferior colliculus or dorsal cochlear nucleus.
CaiIC The CaiIC module implements the Cai et al. (1998) model for neurons of the central nucleus of the inferior colliculus (ICC). While there is wide variation in membrane characteristics between the neurons of this nucleus, and across their population they receive inputs from a number of sources, the Cai et al. IC model focuses primarily on the subpopulation of ICC neurons that are sensitive to interaural time delay (ITD) and respond best to low-frequency auditory inputs.
CaiIC2 The CaiIC2 module simulates individual neurons of the central nucleus of the inferior colliculus (ICC) that are sensitive to interaural time delay (ITD) and respond best to low-frequency auditory inputs. While the input structure is the same as the CaiIC module, and the hyperpolarization conductance for adaptation of Cai et al. (1998) is included, the single-compartment Hodgkin-Huxley model is otherwise that of Rothman and Manis (2003c), with kinetics based on their measurements of three types of K+ channels in the ventral cochlear nucleus (Rothman and Manis 2003a) and additional studies describing the kinetics of INa and Ih elsewhere in the mammalian nervous system.

Originally intended for cochlear implant research, this module takes a multi-channel pulsetrain of currents from the CIS module, multiplies those currents by a supplied weighting matrix, and produces a membrane potential field intended to be used by the auditory nerve IFR module

MSO Cell (Brughera96) The MSOCellBrughera96 module (Brughera et al., 1996) simulates neural discharge patterns of a principal cell in the medial superior olive (MSO). The module is a single-compartment Hodgkin-Huxley neural membrane model very similar to the BushyCellRothman93 module (Rothman et al., 1993). The two differ in that the MSO receives binaural inputs, and in two of their eight ion-channel activation equations.
Simple Signal Generator Simple Signal Generator
Vocoder an N-band vocoder module, which takes envelope information from the EnvelopeExtractor module and multiplies that envelope with N bands of sinusoids at the requested center frequencies
VoigtDCNCell The VoigtDCNCell module implements sets of model dorsal cochlear nucleus (DCN) neurons as in the Zheng and Voigt (2006) model for response maps of the DCN.
ZhouLSO The ZhouLSO module simulates neurons in the lateral superior olive (LSO) that are sensitive to interaural level difference. Each model LSO neuron is a leaky “integrate and fire” model with adaptation. Discharge properties of the model cell suggest that the strength and time constant of adaptation determine four basic response types observed in LSO neurons (Zhou and Colburn 2008).
